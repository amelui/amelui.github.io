Projects

Strands to Top Story Topic Matching
on Stack Overflow

While at The New York Times, I became a Strands fan (and even created a jazz-themed puzzle published on August 8, 2025!). As part of the NYT hackathon, I applied NLP techniques such as TF-IDF keyword weighting and WordNet-based similarity detection to match top-story language against Strands game vocabulary, helping flag potential offensive matches.

Detecting Condescending Comments
on Stack Overflow

Stack Overflow is every programmer's best friend. I experimented using different natural language processing models (Latent Semantic Model, Non-Negative Matrix Factorization) with TF-IDF variations, as well as classification models (kNN, Logistic Regression) to determine condescending comments on Stack Overflow.

Predicting Game Outcomes:
League of Legends

E-Sports is only getting bigger, and I am interested in variables of a game that lead to a winning outcome. Using classification models (kNN, logistic regression, random forests), I found that gold and team experience made the biggest indicators of a win.

Visualizing Asian-American Populations for Asian Supermarket Placement

As a Singaporean abroad, I found the local Asian supermarket very comforting to visit in moments of homesickness. Using Tableau, I made easy-to-understand visualizations of where Asian-American populations live in the U.S., so that future Asian businesses know where they may best serve the community.

Data Exploration for Jazz Bars
Using MTA Dataset

I found a love of jazz when I visited 55 Bar in the city. When coming across the MTA dataset, which houses information about turnstile use in all MTA stations, I thought I would explore heavy foot traffic in the evenings in West Village, as future bar owners can learn where to best place their business.

Webscraping Workshop
for Data Science Club

Our data science club at university was suffering low attendance rates, so I suggested a change from lecture-style meetings to hands-on workshops. I prepared code ahead of time, using a fun example of "Gravity Falls" to increase attention. During the workshop, I walked everyone through code and came up with fun challenges. The workshop had our highest attendance of the year.

A Dive Into TripAdvisor Restaurant Rankings

Singaporean hawker stalls and restaurants are a pillar of our community. However, many family-run businesses don't have the means to hire research for marketing or business development. After webscraping TripAdvisor, I used linear regression to find out how Singaporean restaurants can up their ranking.
